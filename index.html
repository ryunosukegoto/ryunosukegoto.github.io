Ryunosuke Goto, MD（後藤 隆之介）

Pediatrician-scientist

I'm a Pediatrician at the University of Tokyo Hospital and Nagano Children's Hospital. After receiving primary and secondary education in the United Kingdom and the United States, I received my MD from the University of Tokyo in 2019.

As a Pediatrician, I have seen many children and adolescents with physical health, mental health, and developmental (autism spectrum disorder in particular) conditions, and learned that intervening early and at appropriate timings could help prevent the onset of mental and physical illnesses later in life.

Based on my clinical experience, I have conducted numerous studies on social determinants of health as well as mental health and chronic disease of children and adolescents. The goals of my research are to 1. examine the influence of various exposures in throughout the lifecourse (e.g., adverse childhood experiences, socioeconomic status, among others) and their relationship with development, mental health, and chronic disease, and 2. advance precision medicine by tailoring interventions to meet the unique needs of individuals based on their socioeconomic and health circumstances. My work has been published in The Lancet, JAMA Pediatrics, and Pediatrics, among other journals.
